BENGAL TIGER
(Panthera tigris tigris)		 Typical Life Span:
8-10 Years		 Height:
90-110 cm		 Length:
270-310 cm		 Canines:
7.5 to 10 cm long
(Longest among all cats)
  • Mostly found in Indian subcontinent.
  • National animal of India and Bangladesh.
  • Their saliva contains natural antiseptic elements that can prevent wounds from becoming infected.
Conservation Status:
Endangered

ASIATIC LION
(Panthera leo leo)		 Typical Life Span:
16-18 Years		 Height:
107-120 cm		 Length:
282-287 cm		 Weight:
100-190 kg
  • The Asiatic lion is surviving today only in India.
  • Lions are the only cats that live in groups, called prides.
  • The most distinguishing characteristic of the Asiatic lion is the longitudinal fold of skin that runs along its belly. This is absent in African lions.
Conservation Status:
Endangered

AFRICAN ELEPHANT
(Loxodonta)		 Typical Life Span:
60-70 Years		 Height:
2.50-4 m		 Weight:
2270-6350 kg		 Tusks:
1.5-2.4 m long
  • African elephants are distributed in Sub-Saharan Africa.
  • Elephants exhibit a wide variety of behaviors, including those associated with grief, learning, mimicry, art, play, a sense of humor, altruism, etc.
  • African elephants are highly intelligent. They have a very large and highly convoluted neocortex, a trait they share with humans, apes and some dolphin species.
Conservation Status:
Threatened (Endangered + Critically Endangered)
